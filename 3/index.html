GRAPHIC DESIGN IN COLD WAR

During the Cold War, graphic design played a pivotal role in propaganda, serving as a tool for shaping political narratives and influencing public opinion. Posters produced by the United States and the Soviet Union emphasized contrasting ideologies, promoting concepts such as freedom and democracy versus communism and collectivism. These propaganda posters reflected the societal tensions of the era, actively contributing to the ideological struggle between the two superpowers.

CAPITALIST INFLUENCE ON COMMUNIST DESIGN

1. Through typography and typeface.

2. Capitalist design platforms used innovative typography and experimental typeface designs.

3. Commercial companies that want progressive and forward-thinking image used the geometric forms, and minimalist color palettes associated with constructivism to appeal to consumers.

4. Communist design influenced Capitalist design to commodify ideas, turning them into marketing products in the capitalist system.

COMMUNIST INFLUENCE ON CAPITALIST DESIGN

1. Constructivist movement, functionalism, simplicity, and geometric abstraction impacted Capitalist design aesthetics.

2. Capitalist designers were influenced by radicalism, avant-garde constructivism.

3. Capitalist graphic designers’ vibrant/visually striking posters were influenced by communist propaganda art.

4. Bold colors, dynamic composition, and persuasive message techniques of capitalist advertising were used by communists to promote their ideology and system.
