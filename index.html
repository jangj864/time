Graphic Design in Cold War

During the Cold War, graphic design played a pivotal role in propaganda, serving as a tool for shaping political narratives and influencing public opinion. Posters produced by the United States and the Soviet Union emphasized contrasting ideologies, promoting concepts such as freedom and democracy versus communism and collectivism. These propaganda posters reflected the societal tensions of the era, actively contributing to the ideological struggle between the two superpowers.
